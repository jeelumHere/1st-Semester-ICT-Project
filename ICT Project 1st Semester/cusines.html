Delights of Pakistani Cuisine

Explore the rich flavors of every province and city of Pakistan

Punjab Cuisine

Punjab is famous for its rich, flavorful, and hearty dishes. From spicy curries to tandoori delights, the province’s cuisine reflects warmth and festivity.

Butter Chicken

A creamy and aromatic chicken dish, loved across Punjab and beyond. Perfect with naan or rice.

Paya

Paya is a traditional Punjabi delicacy made from slow-cooked trotters, rich in flavor and cherished for its hearty, spicy broth.

Makki ki Roti with Saag

"Makki ki roti", golden and warm, paired with spicy, creamy "sarson ka saag" — a true taste of Punjabi tradition that warms the heart and soul.

Gajar ka Halwa

The rich aroma of ghee, the sweetness of carrots, and the crunch of almonds make Gajar ka Halwa a classic Punjabi dessert loved by all.

Haleem

Slow-cooked Haleem, where meat, lentils, and spices come together in a delicious harmony.

Seekh Kebabs

Tender minced meat, perfectly spiced and grilled on skewers — that’s the magic of Seekh Kebabs.

Sindh Cuisine

Sindh is famous for its flavorful, spicy dishes and seafood delicacies. The cuisine reflects its coastal culture and vibrant spices.

Sindhi Biryani

A fragrant rice dish cooked with tender meat, spices, and herbs, representing Sindh’s rich culinary heritage.

Sindhi Curry

Sindhi Curry: A perfect blend of spice and tang, celebrating the rich culinary heritage of Sindh.

Sai Bhaji

Sai Bhaji: A wholesome Sindhi dish of spinach and lentils, slow-cooked to perfection — healthy, hearty, and full of flavor.

Sindhi Kadhi Chawal

Sindhi Kadhi Chawal: Tangy, spiced gram flour curry with fresh vegetables, served with steamed rice — a wholesome Sindhi classic.

Dal Pakwan

A perfect combination of crunchy bread and hearty lentils — Dal Pakwan, a true taste of Sindh.

Fry Fish

Sindhi Fried Fish: Crispy on the outside, tender inside, marinated with traditional Sindhi spices — a true coastal delight.

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Cuisine

KPK cuisine is known for its meat-rich, aromatic dishes often cooked over wood fire. Chapli kebabs and lamb curries are specialties of this region.

Chapli Kebab

Spicy minced meat kebabs, famous in Peshawar, perfect as appetizers or with naan.

Traditional Lamb Karahi

KPK Lamb Karahi: Tender lamb cooked with fresh tomatoes, green chilies, and aromatic spices — a spicy, flavorful specialty from Khyber Pakhtunkhwa.

Kabuli Pulao

Kabuli Pulao: Fragrant rice cooked with tender lamb, sweet carrots, and raisins — a rich and aromatic KPK specialty.

Peshawari Naan

Peshawari Naan: Soft, fluffy bread stuffed with nuts and raisins — a sweet and savory delight from Peshawar.

Dum Pukht

Savor the magic of Dum Pukht, where every bite tells the story of slow-cooked excellence and culinary artistry.

Namkeen Mandi

KPK’s Namkeen Mandi offers pure flavor — slow-cooked meat, delicate salt, and the unmistakable charm of mountain cuisine.

Balochistan Cuisine

Balochistan offers unique flavors with roasted meats and simple yet rich recipes. Sajji is the signature dish of the province.

Sajji

Whole marinated lamb roasted over open fire. A must-try specialty of Balochistan.

Kaak

Kaak is more than bread — it’s a taste of Baloch hospitality, sturdy, wholesome, and perfect with Sajji or tea.

Balochi Tikka

Balochi Tikka is all about pure, smoky perfection — tender meat grilled over open flames with minimal spices that let the natural flavor shine.

Laghman

Laghman combines delicate spices and fresh ingredients to create a balanced, soulful meal that feels both traditional and modern.

Shorwa

Shorwa carries the essence of tradition, where patience and simplicity transform humble ingredients into a hearty, aromatic delight.

Sajji Pulao

Sajji Pulao brings together the tender, smoky flavors of traditional Sajji with fragrant, perfectly cooked rice — a true taste of Balochistan.
